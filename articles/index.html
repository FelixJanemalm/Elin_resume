Why storytelling is the key to successful brand communication

Once upon a time. The stories that we all know and the characters that we have been carrying with us since the moment we met them. Unsung heroes defeating the odd and overcoming their setbacks. We feel their journey, we share their tears and we celebrate with them in their successes. Sadness from Inside Out is a character that’s have been instrumental in the last few years of my life, presenting a new perspective for me to look on the world and how I interact with it. All stories that captivate us, doesn’t need to be that emotional deep though. The love for some of our modern heroes such as Jake Sully from Avatar and Frodo from Lord of the Rings comes from the viewer’s recognition of themselves in the character, story arch and the situation happening.

What many modern brands have missed is that it doesn’t take high-quality production and visual to create engagement between the audience and your brand. High production quality doesn’t mean anything if you expect the viewer to watch a 3-minute promo of this is why we are the best. What’s in that for them? Say that we have a shoe brand, and the commercial is following the shoes walking around for one minute. Possibly capturing the attention of some that are fans of the cinematography or other aspects of your content, but what is there to engage about for people that are incredibly passionate about shoes that give them value? Absolutely, I won’t ignore the effects of good visuals have and sharing for simply liking the product but I argue that you’re missing opportunities to double or triple your engagement as well as your relationship with your consumers because of the way its framed.

I recently found and read Building a Story Brand by Donald Miller that put a framework of my theories. A brands communication should be, and always be about the consumer. But I´m going deeper and adapting this framework into content for social media.

Looking at creating a narrative that fits with a brand slogan, for example, Loreal Paris Because “You’re worth it”. Endless possibilities with that.
How fascinating and intriguing would it be for Loreal Paris to have a weekly show offering one of their consumers a makeover? Some sassiness and personality from the stylists that are featured every episode, stories that connect from the consumer and genuine interaction between the two would make for a compelling series for people interested in makeup and hair to follow which are the people in Loreal Paris target audience. Eva Longoria, that’s been a spokesperson for them for years could even future.
You could have the full episodes on Youtube, with simply the makeover sequences cut together into a minute for Instagram. Facebook could dive deeper into a portrait of featured consumers with photos and articles.

I leave it with a final endpoint, we are living in a time where everything that is talked about is something. So why not create something that places yourself in the conversation where you want to be.

Let me explain because it’s an easy assumption that your appearance, with that I mean your numbers and following, is what counts on social media and is what gives you all the great opportunities that we see our favourite influencer build a career from. It seems obvious that this is how social media works, but I am here to tell you that your appearance isn’t what matters. Opportunities come from your digital interactions, and how many people that want to interact with you and that has nothing to do with your numbers. That only has to do with your personality and your content. What finally have caught up to the world of social media marketing is the realisation that the numbers lie. 1 million followers don’t mean that 1 million people are actively engaging with that profile. An account with 500 followers, could have a higher engagement % and be more connected to the people that they are communicating with. This should make them more attractive to a brand that is looking for collaboration. If it wasn’t for the appearance, that brands sadly tend to go after.

I am here to argue, that both brands and yourself looking to grow within social media would go further by not looking at everybody else. By thinking of what truly makes your heart bubble and creativity tickle, and stick to that. Stick to what makes you, and not what may look best on the outside. By doing that, the real opportunities will come to you both professional but also personal. Brands won’t grow to the top of their industry by mimicking companies that came before them, it’s by finding their market opening by truly understand their heart which is their customer. A personal account won’t receive nearly as much recognition or opportunity from copying another account because it lacks the feeling of being genuine. Because the truth is that the person that you look up to for their appearance got there because of their heart.

Its all about your heart. I have received so much more, both in terms of genuine connections to business opportunities since I stopped looking at my appearance and starting to focus on what I truly loved doing.
